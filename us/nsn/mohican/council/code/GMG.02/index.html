Legislative History

1. Revenue Allocation Plan approved by Tribal Council on January 17, 1995, in Resolution No. 1461-95. Section 27.8(F) (later 27.7((F)) amended by Resolution 1553-95 on November 7, 1995. Rejected by BIA Minneapolis on February 1, 1996. Section 27.8(F) (later Section GMG.02.07(f)) amended by Resolution No. 012-96, February 20, 1996. Approved by BIA Minneapolis, March 14, 1996.

2. Section GMG.02.03(a), (A)(1), (A)(2), (C), (D), Section GMG.02.04(a), (A)(1), (A)(2), (B)(1), (B)(2), Section GMG.02.05(a), (A)(1), (A)(2), Section GMG.02.06(a), (A)(1), Section GMG.02.07, Section GMG.02.08 [now Section GMG.02.07] (A), (A)(1), (A)(2), (A)(3), (B), (C), (C)(1), (C)(3), (D), (E) amended by Resolution No. 038-2000 on July 10, 2000. Section GMG.02.09 became Section GMG.02.08, Section GMG.02.10 became Section GMG.02.09, Section GMG.02.11 became Section GMG.02.10, Section GMG.02.12 became Section GMG.02.11, Section 27.13 became Section GMG.02.12 by Resolution No. 038-2000. On November 9, 2000 BIA, Indian Gaming Management (Washington DC) determines that the Revenue Allocation Plan does not comply with IGRA and 25 C.F.R. 290. Specifically, the BIA determination affects Section GMG.02.07(b)(2), (C)(4). Further, the BIA determination states that the "plan must include and/or establish a process for dispute resolution…in accordance with 25 C.F.R. 290.12(b)(5)."

3. Sections Section GMG.02.03(e), Section GMG.02.07(a), (B)(2)(a)(b)(c), (C)(4)(5)(6), Section GMG.02.09, Section GMG.02.12(b) amended by Resolution No. 04-2201 on February 14, 2001 in order to address technical issues raised in BIA's November 9, 2000 letter. BIA, Indian Gaming Management, Washington DC approves amendments from February 14, 2001 Resolution No. 04-2201, May 25, 2001.

4. Sections Section GMG.02.07(c), (C)(3); Section GMG.02.07(f) amended by Tribal Council to address the trust agreement; to address encouraging students to obtain their high school diploma or G.E.D.; to address debts owed to the Tribe and the debtor declines Per Capita Payment. Section GMG.02.07(c)(7) newly created to address the trust fund monies if the minor qualified tribal members dies before receiving the trust fund. Sections Section GMG.02.07(g), (G)(1), (G)(1)(a), (G)(1)(b) newly created to permit Tribal Court ordered child support. June 1, 2004, Resolution 026-04. BIA, Indian Gaming Management, Washington DC approves amendments, July 20, 2004.

5. On August 9, 2007, by Resolution No. 056-07, Tribal Council amended by: modifying §Section GMG.02.07(a); Section GMG.02.07(b)(1); Section GMG.02.07(b)(2); Section GMG.02.07(c); Section GMG.02.07(c)(1); Section GMG.02.07(c)(3); Section GMG.02.07(c)(3)(A), (e); deleting the existing §Section GMG.02.07(c)(3)(B) and creating §§Section GMG.02.07(c)(3)(B), (c), and(d); and by creating §§Section GMG.02.07(c)(2)(A), (b), (c), and (d); §§Section GMG.02.07(c)(3)(B), (c), (d), and (f); §§Section GMG.02.07(c)(8), (9), and (10); §Section GMG.02.07(h). BIA, Indian Gaming Management, Washington DC, approves amendments on October 24, 2007.

6. On June 16, 2009, by Resolution No. 066-09, Tribal Council amended by creating new Section GMG.02.01(b), amending Section GMG.02.01(a), and renumbering Section GMG.02.01 to Section GMG.02.01(a). Amendment approved by the BIA on July 20, 2009.

As there were technical issues with the execution of Resolution No. 060-11, it was revoked by the Tribal Council on May 1, 2012. The amendments were readopted on May 1, 2012 by Resolution No. 034-12. deleting "Tribal Council" and inserting "Tribe's Finance Department"; deleting "listed in Section GMG.02.03(b)"; and Amending Section GMG.02.03(d) by inserting "At least sixty days prior to the commencement of each new fiscal year, the"; and Amending Section GMG.02.03(e) by deleting "or its appointed agent" and inserting ", through the tribe's Finance Department,"; deleting "operations or program's generally accepted accounting methods." and inserting "Tribe's policies and procedures and Generally Accepted Accounting Principles."; and Amending Section GMG.02.03(f) by inserting "new"; and Amending Section GMG.02.04(a) (1) by deleting "revise and increase " and inserting "increase or decrease"; deleting "the tribal government operations and programs" and inserting "tribal economic development"; and Amending Section GMG.02.07(f)by deleting "Amounts" and inserting "Obligations"; inserting "In the event that the obligations of the tribal members are non-monetary then the tribal member's per-capita payment will be withheld until the individual satisfies the obligation. If the individual has failed to comply within thirty (30) days, then the tribal member's per-capita payment for that particular year will be returned to the Net Gaming Revenue's General Welfare Fund. Examples include but are not limited to a tribal member refusing to vacate a tribal housing unit, failing to complete community service, etc. In addition to any other tribal remedies, the tribal member's per-capita payment would be withheld.
