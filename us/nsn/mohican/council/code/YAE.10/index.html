Legislative History

Adopted by Tribal Council on September 7, 1999, Resolution Number 057-99.

Approved by BIA November 1, 1999.

Technical changes made in footnote 1, Section YAE.10.02(a), and 65.5(1). Section YAE.10.03(c) added and Appendix A moves the crime reckless driving – cause great bodily harm, felony 346.63(4) from a Category I – Permanent Bar to a Category II – Risk Review Crime on December 6, 1999, Resolution Number 082-99.

Approved by BIA December 17, 1999.

Technical changes made in Section YAE.10.02(a), Section YAE.10.03(a)(4), Section YAE.10.09(a), and Section YAE.10.09(a)(2) adding to "Abuse" definition, allowing for otherwise confidential records to be available for lawful regulatory purposes, and changing a two-year limited background check to a four-year complete background check on March 21, 2000, Resolution Number 09-2000.

Technical changes approved by BIA March 29, 2000.

Wisconsin Department of Health and Family Services approves Tribal Elder/Youth License and Tribe as a Tribal Designated Rehabilitative Review Agency, May 8, 2000.

On November 19, 2002, Tribal Council, by Resolution No. 071-02, amended sections Section YAE.10.06(b) [90 days to 120 days], Section YAE.10.06(b)(2) [90 days to 120 days], Section YAE.10.08(e) [2 years to 4 years], Section YAE.10.09(a)(1) [90 days to 120 days], and Section YAE.10.09(b) clarifying Compliance and Human Resources responsibilities. Approved by BIA December 31, 2002. Wisconsin Department of Health and Family Services notified on January 14, 2003.

On December 16, 2003, Tribal Council, by Resolution No. 056-03, amended Section YAE.10.09 by creating Sections Section YAE.10.09(b)(1), Section YAE.10.09(b)(1)(A), Section YAE.10.09(b)(1)(B) and Section YAE.10.09(b)(2). Approved by BIA on January 22, 2004.

On October 26, 2010, the Tribal Council adopted Resolution No. 01-11 requiring that Elder/Youth licensing requirements apply to persons consistent with requirements under the Wisconsin caregiver law adopted in 2009. This resolution required that caregiver background checks for certified childcare providers, household members, employees, assistants and helpers shall be evaluated and addressed in accordance with the provisions of Chapters 48.685 and 50.065, Wis. Stats., and as enumerated in Chapter DHS 12 APPENDIX A, OFFENSES AFFECTING CAREGIVER ELIGIBILITY, SEPTEMBER 2000, in lieu of the Chapter 65 licensing requirements. This resolution was made effective until Chapter 65 was amended or the resolution was revoked.
